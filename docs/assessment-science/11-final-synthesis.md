# Document 11 — Final Synthesis

**Synthesizes:** [Documents 00–10](./00-security-excellence-model.md).

## 1. Executive summary

CQrityjob's Assessment DNA Framework is a purpose-built scientific
foundation for assessing security professionals, grounded primarily in
established industrial/organizational psychology, high-reliability-
organization (HRO) research, naturalistic decision-making theory, and
operational-domain evidence (aviation CRM, military and police
selection, investigation/intelligence methodology) — not adapted from
generic commercial personality/ability testing. It starts from a
first-principles definition of professional excellence in
high-consequence, trust-dependent work (Document 00), decomposes that
into a master competency model grounded in the platform's existing
Career Intelligence Graph vocabulary (Document 02) and an underlying
Dimension model reconciled across every existing system that already
uses that word (Document 03), specifies which evidence-collection
methods and measurement scales are scientifically appropriate for
which constructs rather than assuming a single universal approach
(Documents 04–05), sets permanent construction and quality principles
for any future assessment content (Document 06), reasons explicitly
about how competency priorities shift across the full range of
security sub-professions without changing the underlying model
(Document 07), draws a hard, structurally-enforced boundary around
AI's role (Document 08), and commits to an ongoing, evidence-based
reliability and validity program rather than a one-time design claim
(Document 09). It is designed to be extended, not replaced, as the
platform grows toward the full Security Intelligence Platform ambition
(Document 10).

## 2. Scientific strengths

- **Starts from excellence, not from convenient constructs** — the
  Document 00 first-principles approach avoids the common failure mode
  of building an assessment from whichever traits are easy to measure
  rather than the ones that actually differentiate outcomes.
- **Explicit trainable-vs-fixed and success-vs-failure distinctions**
  (Document 00 §4–5) are unusually rare in commercial frameworks and
  directly enable honest, useful coaching (Document 08) rather than
  generic development advice.
- **Method-to-construct discipline** (Documents 04–05) — refusing a
  universal scale or a default evidence method is a genuine scientific
  improvement over the authoring-convenience shortcuts common
  elsewhere in the industry, and is enabled at no schema cost by the
  already-locked, per-Question-Version architecture.
- **Non-compensatory treatment of integrity-adjacent competencies**
  (Document 06 §7) directly addresses a well-known weakness of
  simple weighted-average scoring models: a high score elsewhere
  cannot mask a critical integrity concern.
- **Structural, not policy-level, AI boundary** (Document 08 §9) — the
  "AI never decides" commitment is enforced by the absence of a
  writable decision field in the schema itself, which is a materially
  stronger guarantee than a stated policy alone.
- **Reconciles rather than ignores prior art** (Document 03 §5) — the
  framework explicitly accounts for the legacy public assessment and
  the dormant Career Intelligence Graph dimension infrastructure
  instead of creating a competing, contradictory model.

## 3. Potential weaknesses

- **Entirely a-priori at this stage.** Every validity and reliability
  claim in this document set is reasoned from established literature
  and design principles, not yet from CQrityjob's own data — this is
  appropriate for a foundational document but must not be mistaken for
  empirical validation (§4–5 below).
- **Domain and dimension counts (10 domains/~28 competencies, 12
  dimensions) are a reasoned first pass, not empirically derived
  factor structures** — real response data may reveal that some
  proposed dimensions do not separate cleanly (Document 09 §2,
  construct validity) or that the domain grouping in Document 02 needs
  revision.
- **Forced-choice/ipsative scoring methodology** (Document 05) is
  flagged as scientifically preferred for reducing social-desirability
  bias but its scoring-methodology detail is explicitly deferred to
  Phase 2B/scoring design — a real gap between principle and
  implementation-readiness.
- **Dilemma-question and free-text justification scoring** (Document
  04) has an acknowledged, unresolved inter-rater-reliability question
  (Document 09 §3) that must be solved or the method type restricted
  to narrative-only use.
- **Cross-cultural and cross-jurisdictional validity is asserted as a
  requirement, not yet demonstrated** (Document 09 §7) — a real risk
  given the platform's global ambition, and one of the largest gaps
  between this framework's stated scope and its current evidence base.

## 4. Assumptions requiring validation

- That the twelve dimensions (Document 03) are empirically separable
  constructs, not overlapping restatements of a smaller true factor
  structure.
- That the profession-level reasoning in Document 07 (which
  competencies differentiate most per environment) matches real
  performance-outcome data once available — currently expert reasoning
  by analogy to adjacent, published research on comparable occupations,
  not CQrityjob-specific evidence.
- That situational-judgement "most/least effective" keys, once
  authored in Phase 2B, actually match genuine expert consensus for
  this specific industry rather than the item author's individual
  judgement.
- That the self-report weakness pattern documented for D3/D9
  (Document 03 §4, drawn from the general personnel-selection
  literature) replicates specifically in a security-industry
  respondent population, which may differ from the populations
  underlying that general literature.

## 5. Research gaps that should be validated before large-scale rollout

- **Predictive validity data does not yet exist** (Document 09 §2)
  and cannot be shortcut — this is the single largest gap before any
  strong predictive claim should be made to employers.
- **GDPR/retention duration for recurring assessment data** remains an
  explicit open Product Owner decision carried from the locked
  architecture (Document 01 §6) — required before Stage 4/5 scales.
- **Adaptive assessment's reproducibility model** (Document 04, Stage
  6) is unresolved and must be designed before that stage, not
  discovered during it.
- **Bias and differential-item-functioning screening** requires real
  subgroup sample sizes not yet available (Document 09 §5–6).
- **Learning-content-to-outcome validation** (Document 08 §4) — no
  evidence yet connects any specific recommended learning activity to
  measured competency improvement.

## 6. Recommended future academic collaborations

- **Industrial/organizational psychology researchers** with published
  work in high-reliability-organization or security/safety-critical
  occupational contexts, for independent review of Documents 00 and
  02–03 before Phase 2B content authoring proceeds at scale.
- **Psychometricians with situational-judgement-test and item-
  response-theory expertise**, engaged ahead of Stage 6 (Adaptive
  Assessment) specifically, given that stage's calibration
  requirements.
- **Cross-cultural assessment researchers**, engaged ahead of any
  non-Swedish/English-market launch, to design the re-validation
  process required by Document 09 §7 rather than assuming translation
  alone suffices.

## 7. Recommended pilot studies

- **A criterion-validity pilot**: assess a cohort of already-employed
  professionals in one or two initial Role/Environment combinations,
  pair results with independent, existing performance data (supervisor
  ratings, documented incident/commendation history) collected without
  reference to the assessment result, and compute the correlation —
  the first real evidence toward closing the predictive-validity gap
  in §5.
- **A test-retest pilot** on the initial Question Library, at an
  interval short enough to rule out real change, to establish baseline
  reliability estimates per dimension before wide rollout.
- **A face-validity pilot** with both candidates and employer
  reviewers on the first published Blueprint, specifically checking
  whether Document 04's dilemma-question and consistency-check content
  is experienced as intended (decision support) rather than as an
  interrogation (the explicit risk flagged in Document 01 §3 and
  Document 04).

## 8. Recommended psychometric validation strategy

Adopt Document 09's four-phase structure (design-time → early-data →
ongoing → longitudinal) as the platform's standing practice, with two
concrete near-term commitments: (a) no Blueprint Version should be
described to an employer as "validated" until it has passed at least
the early-data internal-consistency and initial bias-screening checks,
not merely design-time content review; (b) a recurring (not one-time)
adverse-impact monitoring process should be operational no later than
Stage 4's exit criteria (Document 10), before Career Intelligence
integration (Stage 5) begins consuming Blueprint-derived results at
scale.

## 9. Recommended Phase 2B — Question Library Design

Phase 2B is content authoring, not architecture or science — this
document recommends its **scope and gating**, not its content (which
remains explicitly out of scope for this document set, per repeated
instruction throughout).

**Recommended scope:** author the minimum Question Library needed to
publish one complete, Document 06 §11-compliant Blueprint Version for
the already-curated launch Role × Environment combination (per the
locked architecture's Phase 1 curation flags), covering all six
foundational dimensions (D1–D6) with the evidence-method diversity
Document 04 requires, explicitly including at least one non-self-
rating method for D3 and D9 per that document's caution.

**Recommended gating before Phase 2B begins:** this document set
(00–11) reviewed and approved as binding reference material for
content authors; a content-authoring workflow that requires each
Question Version to record its target competency/dimension and
evidence-method rationale at authoring time (Document 06 §11's
checklist, operationalized as a real authoring step, not only a
review-time check).

**Recommended gating before Phase 2B's output is treated as
production-ready:** the Document 09 §8 design-time validity checks
(content validity against Documents 02–03, expert review) — not the
statistical checks, which require real response data Phase 2B alone
cannot produce (Stage 4, Document 10).

**Explicitly not part of Phase 2B, per this framework:** any change to
the public 16-question assessment; any numerical Requirement Profile
weighting (Document 07 remains reasoning-only until a future,
separately-scoped content decision); adaptive assessment content
(Stage 6, gated far later); any AI-generated question content without
the same expert-review discipline applied to human-authored content.
